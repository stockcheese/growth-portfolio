Why This Sector, Why Now

China controls ~70% of rare earth mining, ~90% of separation/processing, and ~93% of permanent magnet manufacturing. In 2025, Beijing weaponized this dominance with two waves of export controls ‚Äî the second introducing extraterritorial rules requiring Chinese approval for any magnet worldwide containing even 0.1% Chinese rare earth material. A temporary suspension lasts until November 10, 2026. The clock is ticking.

üèõ US Government Response

$12B Project Vault strategic stockpile (Feb 2, 2026). DoD equity investments in MP Materials ($400M). $277M Commerce Dept funding for USA Rare Earth. $120M EXIM Bank LOI for Critical Metals. NDAA FY2026 bans Chinese rare earth magnets in defense systems by 2027. Section 232 investigation on processed critical minerals launched Jan 15, 2026.

üá®üá≥ China's Rare Earth Weapon

April 2025: Export controls on 7 rare earths + magnets. October 2025: Added 5 more REEs + extraterritorial 0.1% rule. November 2025: Suspended Oct controls for 1 year (expires Nov 10, 2026). April controls on Dy, Tb, Sm still active via licensing. Pattern: escalate ‚Üí truce ‚Üí broken promises ‚Üí escalate.

üìä Market Volatility (Feb 2026)

Feb 2: Project Vault announcement sent rare earth stocks surging. Feb 4: VP Vance "tariff floor" trading bloc proposal triggered 8-15% selloffs on fears of utility-style margin caps. Reuters reported administration walking back price floor guarantees for future deals (MP's existing $110/kg floor intact). Creates potential entry points.

‚è∞ The Structural Thesis

CSIS: "The only long-term solution to the rare earths crisis is to build alternative supply chains that reduce dependence on China." Every broken China deal reinforces Western investment. The pause expires Nov 2026 ‚Äî if tensions flare again, controls snap back instantly. This isn't cyclical; it's a decade-long structural decoupling.
